Marvin Cheung - UX and Visual Design

Brief

  • Create a 5-10 page book on any subject, with at least three of the strategies below.
  • Abstraction, Child-like, Collage, Comics, Omnipresence, Die-cut, Interactive, Encyclopedic, Orientation, Simultaneity, Sketchbook, Wordless”

Constraints

  • Time: 3 weeks
  • Budget: $20/ book

Goals

  • Storytelling without words (tested with 8 people)
  • Exercise in character design, set design, & concept art

Users

  • Reading the physical book
  • Have read the Tempest: explore the thematic issues brought about by the plot, and visualize the play better
  • Haven’t read the Tempest: a standalone comic

Art Style

  • Problem: short timeframe
  • Solution: abstract cartoon style

Character Design

  • Problem: too many (twelve) central characters
  • Solution: use animals as characters, symbols, and exaggerated characteristics

Set Design

  • Problem: the set needs to reflect the time when characters meet
  • Solution: mapping character paths

Layout

  • Problem: no more than 10 pages
  • Solution: long horizontal format with no more than 3 drawings per scene, for 9 scenes + cover page

Binding

  • Problem: achieving a polished look with a $20 budget
  • Solution: to achieve the look, I opted for a hardcover binding. And since perfect binding is easy to rip

  • Problem: achieving a polished look with a $20 budget
  • Solution: Having considered common binding methods such as soft cover perfect binding - too flimpsy, and hard cover perfbect binding - too easy to rip with such a long horizontal format, I decided to use a hard cover screw binding. The strength of the screws and the thicker binding edge will help hold the pages in place.

About Me


Hi, thank you for visiting! I am Marvin, currently partaking in “Applications of Digital Analytics and Management Strategies in UX Processes”, a 5 week workshop (Nov-Dec) privately organized by Dan James, who previously held positions at BCG (Consultant), Goldman Sachs (Analyst), and Capital One Labs (Director of Product Management). During which, I am able to develop a real life product from ideation to launch, with a focus on using marketing analytics, usability tests, as well as financing strategies to validate and enhance design. It covers all things design related for a startup from branding and web-deisgn, to app design. It aligns with my interest in problem solving with design, which is what motivated me to transition into UX.

In my last three years as a college student, I spent a lot of time exploring different fields, honing my design capabilities along the way. I learnt to manage semester-long projects (HKU Architecture 2016-2018) - to handle photography professionally (New York Fashion Week 2018 F/W Workshop) - the conventions of branding (Constant, Creative Intern Summer 2018) - as well as the fundamentals of UX (General Assembly UX Design Circuit Summer 2018). Majoring in Illustration at Parsons School of Design enabled a lot of these explorations, the way it encourages me to experiment with visual communication.
