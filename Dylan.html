Marvin Cheung - UX and Visual Design

Brief

  • Adrian approached me with an insight he wanted a usable digital solution to: "Why are we collecting paper receipts to claim business expenses, when everybody is paying digitally with WeChat Pay and AliPay [in China] ?"

Constraints

  • time: 6 weeks
  • budget: none

Goals

  • Developing an insight into a product
  • Learning user research methods
  • Exploring how branding fits into the UX workflow
  • Working with a client on a UX project

Users

  • in their early 20s trying to gain financial confidence so that they can support their ambitions

Context

  • Developed under the mentorship of Jason Early, whose past clients include Google and NASA


Trying to integrate branding into the UX workflow that will result in minimum waste. This essentially means that to save time, concepts in work products are fully developed but not fully rendered, separating the concepts from the execution.

Realizing that problem - solution - value are different parts that need addressing. A solution needs to be applied in order to add value.

Attempting to identify what is beneficial and what is possible

Brainstorming the features necessary to support the solutions identified. Solutions elaborated in slides below.

User flows, wireframes, and post-interview notes on wireframes.

Brainstorming ways of representing information, and experimenting with both abstract and literal ways

Responding to interviews and refining the brief for the diagrams

Requesting as little information as possible and beginning the app experience as soon as possible. All the orientation elements are optional after the 3rd slide.

Solutions

Bringing financial confidence to users by helping users understand their finances. With the app, users can

  1. check transactions from accounts
  2. track their overall spending
  3. begin financial planning by customizing their goals.

About Me


Hi, thank you for visiting! I am Marvin, currently partaking in “Applications of Digital Analytics and Management Strategies in UX Processes”, a 5 week workshop (Nov-Dec) privately organized by Dan James, who previously held positions at BCG (Consultant), Goldman Sachs (Analyst), and Capital One Labs (Director of Product Management). During which, I am able to develop a real life product from ideation to launch, with a focus on using marketing analytics, usability tests, as well as financing strategies to validate and enhance design. It covers all things design related for a startup from branding and web-deisgn, to app design. Best of all, it aligns with my interest in problem solving with design, which is what motivated me to transition into UX.

In my last three years as a college student, I spent a lot of time exploring different fields, honing my design capabilities along the way. I learnt to manage semester-long projects (HKU Architecture 2016-2018) - to handle photography professionally (New York Fashion Week 2018 F/W Workshop) - the conventions of branding (Constant, Creative Intern Summer 2018) - as well as the fundamentals of UX (General Assembly UX Design Circuit Summer 2018). Majoring in Illustration at Parsons School of Design enabled a lot of these explorations, the way it encourages me to experiment with visual communication.
