Does including branding in the UX workflow actually make the proccess leaner?

In order to reduce waste - anything not used in the MVP, people save visual design for last. During the making of Dylan, I explored the pros and cons of beginning branding earlier. Dylan is an iOS app designed for Adrian To to help users gain financial confidence.

Wordless Shakespeare - How do you tell a story without words?

Given the opportunity to create an experimental comic, I thought about wordless comics. For this project, I took a hallmark of western literature - shakespearean plays, and created a 10-page wordless comic to test the limitations of visual communication. Inspired by the emoji-based "Macbeth #killingit" from "OMG Shakespeare".

How do you bridge the gap between personas and user flow?

UX to me is in part about balancing competing needs of users on limited screen real estate, but I did not feel that current models such as sitemap, and userflow, directly relate personas and in-product experiences. As such, I endeavoured to find a bridge that will help us think about how user journeys translate into the structure of a digital system.

About Me


Hi, thank you for visiting! I am Marvin, a student at the Parsons School of Design, and I enjoy designing digital systems.

Currently, I am partaking in “Applications of Digital Analytics and Management Strategies in UX Processes”, a 5 week workshop (Nov-Dec) privately organized by Daniel James, who previously held positions at BCG (Consultant), Goldman Sachs (Analyst), and Capital One Labs (Director of Product Management). During which, I am able to develop a real life product from ideation to launch, with a focus on using marketing analytics, usability tests, as well as financing strategies to validate and enhance design. It covers all things design related for a startup from branding and web-deisgn, to app design. Best of all, it aligns with my interest in problem solving with design, which is what motivated me to transition into UX.

In my last three years as a college student, I spent a lot of time exploring different fields, honing my design capabilities along the way. I learnt to manage semester-long projects (HKU Architecture 2016-2018) - to handle photography professionally (New York Fashion Week 2018 F/W Workshop) - the conventions of branding (Constant, Creative Intern Summer 2018) - as well as the fundamentals of UX (General Assembly UX Design Circuit Summer 2018). My major in illustration at Parsons School of Design supports my experimentation in visual communication.
